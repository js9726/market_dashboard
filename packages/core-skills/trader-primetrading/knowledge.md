# Alex Desjardins (@PrimeTrading_) — methodology

Six core principles: (1) Market phase overrides all setups. (2) Price structure and price action override all indicators. (3) No breakouts — edge is exclusively in pullbacks to 21dma. (4) Early unrealized gains are cushion that funds subsequent adds. (5) Defense is offense — small drawdowns compound; large ones don't recover. (6) Play process, not outcome.

## Setup recognition

**The 4 core 21dma behaviors** (these are the only recognized setups):

1. **Pullback into rising 21dma** — healthy trend continuation; primary entry signal.
2. **Reclaim & Backtest** — stock broke below 21dma, reclaimed it, and backtests from above; bullish.
3. **Reject & Higher Low** — price rejected at 21dma but holds a higher low; trend may still be intact; watch, don't necessarily enter.
4. **Reject & Lower Low** — failed reclaim, trend breaking down; reduce/exit exposure.

**Pullback variants within behaviors 1 and 2:**
- "Liquid Leaders 21dma Pullback" is the primary scan-driven entry type.
- Price must be coiling (contraction in last 5 days) before the entry attempt.

**Out of scope — explicitly NOT his style:**
- Breakout entries (breakout chasing is categorically excluded from this system).
- Any setup not anchored to the 21dma structure.
- Entries in stocks not in the Liquid Leaders universe (except Episodic Pivot, which has its own criteria above).

## Stops & exits

- **Stops are soft and structural** — defined by 21dma-structure, not a fixed percentage of price.
- A close meaningfully below the 21dma-structure signals thesis change; reduce or exit.
- Behavior 4 (Reject & Lower Low) is the primary exit trigger.
- **Trim 1/3 at 2R**; trail remaining 2/3 with the 21dma-structure as a rising stop.
- **Earnings**: exit unless open cushion exceeds the implied move. Never hold through earnings by default.
- No fixed-percent stops. No scaling into losers.

- Start with 10–20% of risk budget on initial entry; add 2–3 times as the trade proves itself.
- Cushion from early unrealized gains funds adds — no margin required.
- Active basket: 30–40 names in a strong market (concentrated, high-RS names only).

## Psychology & discretion

Alex's psychological framework reduces to three words: **Patience. Discipline. Conviction.** Rules are not a rigid checklist — experience converts them into a probabilistic lens. Discretion is earned through screen time and past trade analysis, not emotion. Key operating principles: never force trades in choppy or no-setup markets; do not chase extended moves (missing the first 2% of a move is better than chasing and getting stopped out); trim at 2R to manage open heat actively; trail exits with structure rather than predicting trend ends. Hard drawdown guardrails exist — daily/weekly loss limits prevent the trader death spiral of chasing losses with bigger size. The long-term goal is sustainable 50–100% annual returns, not 300–400% YTD spikes followed by blowups.

---

## Anti-patterns

The following behaviors are explicitly contrary to Alex's system:

- **Breakout chasing** — buying a stock breaking out above resistance; this system has no breakout entries.
- **Extended entry** — entering when price is more than 1× ATR away from the 21dma.
- **Holding through earnings** — binary event risk; exit unless cushion exceeds implied move.
- **Illiquid names** — any stock below $250M daily liquidity or below $10B market cap (outside Episodic Pivot rules).
- **Fixed-percent stops** — stops must be structural (21dma-based), not arbitrary percentage cuts.
- **Scaling into losers** — adds are only made when the trade proves itself; never average down.
- **Trading broken-market phases** — entering new positions during Breakdown phase; market phase overrides all setups.
- **Excluded sectors** — China/HK, Biotech, Defensive, Real Estate, Healthcare, Energy, Financials.

### The Decoupling Phase (new, 2026-05-19)

A distinct sub-phase during market recovery that Alex explicitly trades around:

> "Expecting some decoupling with chop as LLs build their right side and base. Digest, etc."

**What it looks like:** Indices chop sideways or grind weakly while leading stocks quietly hold above their 21dma-structure and build the right side of a base.

**How to trade it:**
- Do NOT force index-correlated momentum trades — chop will stop you out
- Watch which stocks show **relative strength during the chop** — those are the ones to own when momo returns
- Rotate out of names below 21dma-structure even if market "isn't broken" — free up NER for the real leaders
- "If a name isn't showing [RS] here, I don't mind trading a bit more during this transition to position myself properly in the right merchandise"

**"Right Merchandise" principle:** The whole game during this phase is being in the right names *before* momentum resumes. Leaders showing RS during chop = the ones that will lead the next leg up.

## Daily Market Breadth Read (Alex's Framework)

Six instruments Alex monitors every day to assess market health and timing (source: 2026-05-19 X post):

| Instrument | What to watch | Bullish signal | Red flag |
|---|---|---|---|
| **QQQ** | Range structure | Holding prior range → sideways base building | Losing the range level |
| **QQQE** | Equal-weight Nasdaq | At or above yesterday's high | Rolling over while QQQ holds |
| **Credit Spreads** | vs rising 21dma-structure | Above 21dma, trending down | Rejected at initial break zone → spike through = big red flag |
| **VIX** | Inside vs outside structure | Tight/inside structure | Expanding out of structure |
| **BTC** | Risk appetite proxy | Tightening into structure | Breaking down |
| **LLs (Liquid Leaders)** | 21dma-structure zone | Tapping 21dma on deep MCO oversold = bounce signal | Breaking 21dma with no oversold reading |

**Credit Spreads key level:** The "initial break zone where the trend started" is the most important level. A rejection there is normal; a spike *through* it signals a regime change to risk-off. Eyes on it always.

**MCO oversold bounce signal:** When Liquid Leaders (Alex's focus universe) tap their 21dma-structure on a **-3σ MCO reading** (three standard deviations below normal), that is an extreme oversold condition — the type that has historically sparked sharp relief bounces. Alex used exactly this on 2026-05-19 to read that "we might have seen the worst of the selling."

## NER Rotation Protocol

NER = Net Exposure Ratio (net long minus net short as % of account).

When NER is too high (over-margined), Alex's priority order for cuts:
1. **First to go:** Any position trading *below* 21dma-structure (thesis broken regardless of market view)
2. **Second:** Sector laggards — e.g. software names underperforming on a day the group should lead
3. **Third:** Names with poor closes (tail candles, closing in lower half of range)
4. **Keep or add:** Names at *rising* 21dma-structure with RS intact

> "Not because I think the market is in a bad spot — I just can't allow myself to DD more, and more importantly, I want to free up room to focus on the REAL good leaders."

The goal is not de-risking the market — it is concentrating into higher-quality setups. Cutting a below-structure name to fund an at-structure leader is rotation, not fear.

- Soft stops: defined by 21dma-structure, not fixed %
- First target: trim 1/3 at **2R**
- Remaining 2/3: trail with 21dma-structure as rising stop
- Earnings: exit unless cushion exceeds implied move

# Alex's Trading Psychology Reflections

## Overview

This source is a collection of reflections — not rules, but hard-won insights from Alex's own trading experience. They address the gap between knowing the system and executing it consistently. (source: Alex's Trading Psychology Reflections.md)

---

## The Core Trio

> **Patience. Discipline. Conviction.**

Every other psychological principle reduces to one of these three. Without the trio, a good system becomes a bad one through execution failures.

---

## Key Reflections (Selected)

### On P&L Fixation
Stop obsessing over daily P&L. It shifts focus from process quality to outcome, corrupting future decisions. Measure on **process adherence**, not numbers.

### On Superperformance
The biggest winners are identified and entered **early**, before consensus catches on. Waiting for confirmation from everyone means arriving after the best risk/reward is gone.

### On Open Heat
**Open heat** = the unrealized drawdown from an open trade's peak. Manage it actively:
- Trim at 2R to reduce heat
- Don't give back large open gains chasing perfect exits

### On Trend Riding
"Ride trends, don't guess ends." Use moving average structure to trail exits rather than predicting when the trend ends. Most big winners are exited too early.

### On Defense
A -50% drawdown requires a +100% recovery. Small drawdowns compound. Large drawdowns don't recover. Defense is the most underrated edge.

### On FOMO
Missing the first 2% of a move is better than chasing it extended and getting stopped out. Patience for the next setup always beats chasing the last one.

### On Quiet Markets
When there are no good setups: **do not force trades**. Use the time to:
- Read and study
- Run deep PTA (past trade analysis)
- Analyze prior year's best performers
- Recharge mental capital

### On Adaptability
Markets change character. The trader who can't adapt when conditions shift is the one who eventually blows up. Flexibility must be grounded in experience, not emotion.

# 21dma Structure

**Summary**: The 21-day moving average (21dma) is the core structural anchor in Alex's swing trading system — price action relative to it determines entries, stops, and exposure decisions.

**Sources**: Alex's Swing Trading System.md, Alex's scans (TradersLab).md, Trading termsconcepts GLOSSARY.md

**Last updated**: 2026-04-23

---

## What It Is

The 21dma (also called 21ema in some contexts) acts as a dynamic support/resistance level for leading stocks in an uptrend. When a stock is trending, institutional buyers repeatedly accumulate near the 21dma, making it a reliable battleground between supply and demand.

Alex treats the 21dma not as a line but as a **structural zone** — an elastic area where price can overshoot before finding support (source: Trading termsconcepts GLOSSARY.md).

## The 4 Core Behaviors

Price interaction with the 21dma-structure follows four patterns (source: Alex's Swing Trading System.md):

1. **Pullback into rising 21dma** — healthy trend continuation; primary entry signal
2. **Reclaim & Backtest** — stock broke below 21dma, reclaimed it, and backtests from above; bullish
3. **Reject & Higher Low** — price rejected at 21dma but holds a higher low; trend may still be intact
4. **Reject & Lower Low** — failed reclaim, trend breaking down; reduce/exit exposure

## Entry Criteria

- Price must be within **0 to 1x ATR** of the 21dma (source: Alex's scans — Liquid Leaders 21dma Pullback scan)
- 21ema and 10wma must be **advancing** (rising slope confirms uptrend)
- **Daily closing range > 10%** (stock closing in upper range shows demand)
- **Price contraction** in last 5 days (coiling before next move)
- **Earnings 7+ days away** (avoids binary event risk)

## Stops

Stops are "soft" — based on 21dma-structure rather than fixed percentages. A close meaningfully below the 21dma-structure signals the thesis has changed. (source: Alex's Swing Trading System.md)

## MCO/MCSI for Timing

The McClellan Oscillator (MCO) and McClellan Summation Index (MCSI) are used alongside 21dma-structure for market timing:
- MCO signals **short-term** overbought/oversold
- MCSI confirms **trend direction** and breadth
- QQQE (equal-weight Nasdaq-100) is the preferred breadth proxy over QQQ (source: Alex's Swing Trading System.md)

### -3σ MCO Oversold Bounce Signal (added 2026-05-19)

When Alex's Liquid Leaders (his focus universe) tap their 21dma-structure **at the same time** the MCO prints a **-3 standard deviation** reading, this is an extreme oversold condition:

> "LLs tapped the 21dma-structure area today on a -3σ MCO oversold reading — that's deep deep oversold and what sparked today's bounce." — @PrimeTrading_, 2026-05-19

Significance: -3σ MCO is rare (a few times per year). When it coincides with leaders testing the 21dma rather than breaking it, the probability of a sharp bounce is high. This is a buy signal for initiating or adding to positions already above structure, NOT for catching falling knives below structure.

## Credit Spreads as a Risk Regime Signal (added 2026-05-19)

Credit Spreads (corporate bond yield spreads vs Treasuries) are monitored relative to their own 21dma-structure:

| Credit Spread condition | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| Above 21dma-structure, spreads *falling* | Risk-on, institutional confidence returning |
| Above 21dma-structure, spreads *rising* | Caution — credit market stress building |
| Rejected at "initial break zone" (where spread trend started) | Key resistance — potential reversal spot, watch carefully |
| Spikes *through* the initial break zone | **Big red flag** — credit market regime change, reduce risk aggressively |

The "initial break zone where the trend started" is the most important level. A rejection there is normal and tradable. A clean spike through it signals a regime change to risk-off and should trigger immediate NER reduction.
