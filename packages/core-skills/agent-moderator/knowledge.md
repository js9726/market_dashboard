## Conviction Scoring Model (primary — supersedes the equal-weight persona average)

Adopted 2026-06-15 (operator directive). The headline verdict for any ticker or trade is a single **Conviction Score /100**, weighted toward the *setup that actually works* and *entry-readiness* — not an equal-weight average of the seven personas.

| Component | Weight | Rewards |
|---|---|---|
| **Setup** | /40 | Does the chart match a *proven setup that works* (`BO-CB`, `BO-VCP`, `EP-FRESH`, `POST-GAP-VCP`, Power-of-3, first `PB-21EMA`/`MA-PULLBACK`) AND is it a clean instance — tight base, volume dry-up then expansion, right location near the pivot/highs? Loose/wide base, late/extended, `PARABOLIC`, `EP-SECOND`, or wrong location score low. |
| **Entry** | /30 | Is there an actionable trigger now with a tight stop (the risk-management floor: wider of 1.5×ATR or the structural low) and R:R >= 2? Mid-base / no-trigger / chase scores low. |
| **Theme + Leadership** | /20 | RS Rating (leadership), thematic tailwind, and a fresh catalyst. Use the true RS Rating where available. |
| **Market Sentiment** | /10 | Regime + event gate: a confirmed uptrend adds, fighting the tape or an imminent macro event (FOMC/CPI) subtracts. Mostly common across names on a given day. |

**Verdict bands**: GO >= 75 - WATCH 50-74 - PASS < 50.

**The seven personas now FEED the Setup score; they are no longer averaged.** Use the per-persona rubric below to judge *which* setup is present and how clean it is (e.g. a textbook breakout scores high for @markminervini / @SRxTrades / @Qullamaggie, which lifts the Setup sub-score). The persona spread is now a *style fingerprint* (which trader you most resemble), not the headline number. A name can be an elite leader (high Theme) yet still PASS if Setup+Entry are not ready — that is the intended discrimination (e.g. a parabolic theme leader, or an elite-RS name mid-pullback with no trigger).

### Theme /20 catalyst-source calibration (2026-07-10)

Theme points are not awarded for a vague story. Before a ticker receives high Theme/20, complete ticker-catalyst-analysis enough to identify the business, recent catalyst, three-month event trail, insider/institutional flow, peer/sector trend, next-30-day catalysts, analyst changes, and source gaps.

Medical, healthcare, biotech, clinical-stage pharma, and FDA-driven moves are special cases:

- Treat broad medical/biotech strength as a **rotation/speculation indicator** first, not an automatic long theme.
- Award high Theme/20 only when the catalyst is source-backed, the company stage is clear (commercial product vs clinical-stage), the sector/peer move confirms, and the event risk is acceptable.
- Cap Theme/20 or keep WATCH when the move is binary FDA/clinical-trial risk, the product is hard to evaluate, the chart is just a sector sympathy pop, or insider selling appears into the highs.
- Insider buying by CEO/founder/executives is a stronger positive signal in this bucket than normal; absence of visible insider data is a source gap, not a positive.

This aligns the local style stack: Qullamaggie calls biotech EPs harder than earnings EPs, Jeff Sun excludes biotechs from his post-market scan to remove gap risk, and Alex/PrimeTrading excludes biotech/healthcare from his liquid-leader universe.

### Metric inputs (match the operator's TradingView "Swing Data" + MA/ADR indicators)

Score the components from these live metrics (pull from OpenD; cross-check the operator's indicator panel):

- **ADR%(20)** = `100 x (mean(high/low, 20) - 1)` — tradeable range (Qullamaggie likes ~4%+; very low ADR = slow mover, caps explosiveness even on a clean Setup).
- **RVol** — relative volume. The operator's indicator uses a **50-day** lookback; OpenD `volume_ratio` uses **10-day** — state which you used. Breakout/EP wants a surge (>150%); a pullback wants contraction (<100%). **Intraday caveat:** TradingView's `relative_volume_10d_calc` is *cumulative-so-far-today* against a full-day average, so mid-session it reads far too low (2026-07-06 ~11:06 ET: WULF 1.07x TV vs 3.9x live). OpenD `volume_ratio` (量比) is a per-minute rate and is already time-of-day-adjusted — prefer it live. When you only have the TV field intraday, project it to a full session (÷ the fraction of the day's volume expected by now) before judging surge vs contraction; never call a mover "low RVOL" off a raw cumulative reading. See a-list-gate-and-screener.
- **U/D ratio (50)** = `sum(up-vol,50)/sum(dn-vol,50)` — accumulation (>1; strong >1.5).
- **% off 52W high / % above 52W low** — Minervini wants within ~25% of the high.
- **Avg $ Vol (50)** = `SMA(vol,50) x close` — liquidity gate (Alex $250M+, Jeff $1B+ tiers).
- **RS Rating** — leadership. The operator's chart shows the true Fred/IBD **1-99 RS Rating**; an OpenD/SPY weighted-perf **proxy** is NOT the same scale (the real one needs TradingView `seed_fred6725_rs_rating`). Prefer the true RS Rating when given; label any proxy as a proxy.
- **Stage check (2026-07-09)** — before any bounce/stage label caps the Setup score, verify: 50EMA slope, price vs 50EMA, RS Rating. **Above a rising 50EMA with RS ≥ ~90 = Stage-2 leader in a correction** (a −20-30% post-EP digestion in a high-ADR name is normal), never `STAGE4-BOUNCE` — algo pattern labels are computed from short windows and mislabel post-EP digestion. See the Lane-3 early-entry doctrine in a-list-gate-and-screener and the 2026-07-09 calibration in entry-methods.
- **Stop basis for Entry/30 (2026-07-09)** — the Entry component's risk/R:R must be computed from the **pattern-appropriate stop**: trigger-day LoD or wedge/structure low for reclaim and wedge-break entries, pullback low for `PB-21EMA`, higher-low for day-2+ breakouts. A mechanical 10-day swing low overstates risk (~4× on HPE 2026-07-09) and silently converts valid early entries into PASS.

The extension/liquidity pre-screen (trade-analyser Step 2.4) still runs first and caps the Setup score: extended/late-stage base with no fresh catalyst, or insufficient liquidity for size, cannot be GO. See risk-management for the per-setup stop-distance floor and entry-methods for the "pullback is the setup forming, not weakness" calibration. See a-list-gate-and-screener for the GO-gate doctrine: RVOL is setup-conditional (breakout/EP >=150%, pullback <=100%), **not** a universal floor, and the A-list must screen both a breakout and a pullback lane.

## The Seven Trader Styles (Jie Sheng's Framework)

Each trade is scored 0-10 per trader style across three dimensions:

- **Entry Quality** (0-4 points)
- **Risk Management** (0-3 points)
- **Setup Alignment** (0-3 points)

Each trader section also includes a **SKIP IF** block, a negative checklist used by the `trade-analyser` skill to disqualify a trade quickly when it is the wrong setup category for that trader.

### 1. @markminervini

**Style**: SEPA (Specific Entry Point Analysis) / Superperformance

Key characteristics:

- Stage 2 uptrend stocks only.
- Trend-template gate: price above key moving averages, RS leadership, and proximity to highs.
- VCP (Volatility Contraction Pattern) as setup precondition.
- Tight bases before expansion.
- Very strict entry discipline: no loose price action and no chasing above the pivot.

See mark-minervini-sepa.

**SKIP IF**:

- Stock is not in a clear Stage 2 uptrend.
- Base is loose, wide, or not VCP-tight.
- Entry is far above the pivot point.
- Stock fails the trend template.
- Relative strength leadership is absent.

### 2. @Clement_Ang17

**Style**: Swing Trading + Superperformance

Key characteristics:

- Liquid leaders focus.
- Pocket pivots as entry signals.
- Rides trend moves with trailing moving averages.
- Emphasis on not cutting winners early.
- Journal-driven correction of behavioral leakages after strong equity-curve runs.

Real-world example: May 2025 - MTD +33.5%, YTD +75%. Mistakes in chop phase documented in clement-ang-may-2025-recap. (source: Post by @Clement_Ang17 on X.md)

**SKIP IF**:

- Stock is illiquid and cannot absorb intended size cleanly.
- No pocket-pivot signal or RVol confirmation is missing at entry.
- Price action is choppy, not in a clean trend.
- Market is in a chop phase per his May 2025 lesson.
- The trade is driven by dopamine-chasing or revenge behavior.

### 3. @jfsrev (Jeff Sun)

**Style**: Mechanical / Systematic

Key characteristics:

- Predefined sell rules, with little discretion at exit.
- RVOL confirmation required at entry.
- ATR% extension from 50-MA for avoiding late entries and scaling out.
- VARS (Volatility Adjusted Relative Strength) instead of traditional RS.
- Tight entry: LoD distance should stay below 60% ATR.
- A-rated Focus List process before live execution.

(source: The Complete Traders' Guide.md)

**SKIP IF**:

- LoD distance is greater than 60% of ATR.
- Biotech from the post-market scan; use IBB/XBI exposure rather than single-name gap risk unless the operator explicitly accepts it.
- RVOL is below 100% at entry.
- Setup requires discretionary exit.
- Stock fails VARS/RS rank threshold.
- ATR% extension from 50-MA is already too high for a fresh entry.

### 4. @TedHZhang

**Style**: Portfolio Manager / Institutional Growth

Key characteristics:

- High-quality growth focus.
- Theme/catalyst, fundamentals, and technicals should agree.
- Longer holding periods than pure day/swing scalps.
- Position sizing appropriate for a portfolio, not just a single chart.
- Emphasis on fundamental quality + price structure + portfolio fit.

See ted-zhang-institutional-momentum.

**SKIP IF**:

- Setup is short-term momentum, not a multi-week or multi-month thesis.
- Fundamentals do not support at least a B-quality growth thesis.
- Theme or catalyst is absent.
- Position size ignores portfolio concentration or liquidity.
- Liquidity is insufficient for institutional-style sizing.

### 5. @SRxTrades

**Style**: Technical Swing + Momentum

Key characteristics:

- Two entry methods: breakout or MA pullback.
- 8/21/50 EMA system for market filter and trailing stops.
- Volume confirmation critical at entry.
- 4-tranche trimming approach.
- Best environment: strong trending markets.

(source: The blueprint to consistently making $10km+ in trading.md)

**SKIP IF**:

- 8/21/50 EMAs are not stacked.
- Volume does not confirm at entry.
- Market is in "breakdown" phase per breadth filter.
- Setup is neither a breakout nor an MA pullback.

### 6. @PrimeTrading_ (Alex)

**Style**: Momentum + Price Action

Key characteristics:

- 21dma-structure as primary anchor.
- No breakouts: edge is in pullbacks only.
- Concentrated baskets of 30-40 liquid leaders.
- Soft structure-based stops.
- Universe filtered to liquid, high-RS names.
- Market phase overrides all individual setups.

See persona-primetrading. (source: Alex's Swing Trading System.md)

**SKIP IF**:

- Trade is a breakout.
- Stock is in an excluded universe bucket for Alex's scan: biotech, healthcare, defensive, real estate, energy, financials, China/HK, or other low-fit sector.
- Stock is illiquid or low-RS.
- Stop placement requires fighting the 21dma structure.
- Entry is on a fresh-EP day rather than a later post-EP base.
- Market phase is broken and the trade requires pressing new risk.

### 7. @Qullamaggie

**Style**: Momentum Breakouts + Episodic Pivots

Key characteristics:

- Trades a small number of repeatable setups: common breakouts, episodic pivots, and parabolic short/long setups.
- Breakout setup requires a strong prior move, tight 2-week to 2-month consolidation, and range expansion.
- EP setup requires a fresh catalyst, ideally 10%+ gap, and huge volume near the open.
- Entry normally uses opening-range highs or a clean daily breakout level.
- Stop is normally low of day; should not exceed 1x to 1.5x ATR/ADR.
- Usually risks 0.25-1.0% of account; typical position is 10-20%; avoids more than 30% overnight in one name.

(source: qullamaggie-breakouts-episodic-pivots)

**SKIP IF**:

- This is a "second EP" within months of a prior major run.
- Stop-to-LoD is wider than 1.5x ATR or 1.5x ADR.
- Gap is below 5% on the EP day, or below 10% on a textbook EP.
- Stock already had a 3-6 month run before the gap.
- EP is into overhead supply or fails to hold the opening range.
- Biotech with binary catalyst.
- Trade is a pullback to an MA.

# Mark Minervini SEPA

**Summary**: Mark Minervini's SEPA / Superperformance style is a strict Stage 2 growth-momentum framework built around trend-template stocks, relative strength, tight VCP bases, and precise pivot entries.

**Sources**: trader-style-research-2026-06-11.md, trade-journal-analyser.md

**Last updated**: 2026-06-11

---

## Core Philosophy

Minervini-style trading starts with selectivity. The stock must already be in a confirmed Stage 2 uptrend before the setup is considered. The Trend Template acts as a gate: price above the 150-day and 200-day moving averages, the 150-day above the 200-day, long-term averages rising, price above the 50-day, price well above the 52-week low, price near the 52-week high, and RS rating at least 70 with preference for 80-90+. (source: trader-style-research-2026-06-11.md)

The style does not hunt cheap stocks or early bottoms. It buys strength from contraction: a leading stock builds a tight base, supply dries up, and price breaks through a defined pivot with institutional demand.

## Setup Recognition

The best Minervini-aligned setup has:

| Requirement | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| Market stage | Stock is in Stage 2 uptrend |
| Trend template | Moving averages stacked and rising |
| Relative strength | RS leadership, ideally near new highs |
| Base quality | Tight VCP or orderly contraction, not loose chop |
| Entry location | At or near the pivot, not extended above it |
| Volume | Volume dries up in contraction, expands on breakout |

The VCP matters because it shows volatility contraction and supply absorption before expansion. A stock can be fundamentally exciting, but if the base is loose or the pivot has already been chased, it should not score highly for this profile.

## Risk And Trade Management

Minervini-style risk starts before entry. The pivot must be close enough that a stop can be placed logically without oversizing risk. A late entry damages both R/R and emotional discipline.

Score risk higher when:

- The entry is close to the pivot.
- The stop is pre-defined below the pivot/base failure area.
- Position size is scaled to the actual stop distance.
- The trade is skipped if the chart fails the trend template.

Score risk lower when:

- Entry is far above the pivot.
- The base is wide and requires a loose stop.
- The trader buys before the setup is complete.
- The stock is below key moving averages or in Stage 1/3/4.

## Scoring Rubric

| Dimension | High score | Low score |
|---|---|---|
| Entry Quality | Pivot entry from tight VCP/base in Stage 2 | Chased entry, loose base, or bottom-fish |
| Risk Management | Stop and size defined before entry | Stop too wide, arbitrary, or ignored |
| Setup Alignment | Trend template plus RS plus contraction | Missing trend template, no RS, no VCP |

## SKIP IF

- Stock is not in a clear Stage 2 uptrend.
- Price fails the trend template.
- Base is loose, wide, or not VCP-tight.
- Entry is extended materially above the pivot.
- RS leadership is absent.

## Alternative Early Entry Pivots

Alternative early entry pivots are areas where risk can be managed before the traditional base pivot. The source says these pivots should be well off the lows of the structure, but not so near the highs that they are simply the general base pivot. (source: 3 Early Entry Techniques To Improve Win Rate.md)

Entry is a tight multi-day period before the general base pivot. Exit is below the lows of that tight period, ideally within roughly 3-5% of entry. (source: 3 Early Entry Techniques To Improve Win Rate.md)

## Power Of 3

Power of 3 occurs when a stock moves from below its 10, 21, and 50-day **simple** moving averages to above them in a single session (live article specifies SMAs; downtrending MAs decrease the setup's probability). The source prefers horizontal moving averages, decreasing volume while consolidating below the MAs, and increasing volume as price moves through them. (source: 3 Early Entry Techniques To Improve Win Rate.md)

The entry is a break above the consolidation highs when the bunched moving averages sit near that same point. Risk is generally below the consolidation lows or on repeated closes back below the moving averages. (source: 3 Early Entry Techniques To Improve Win Rate.md)

## Launch Pad

Launch Pad is a follow-on setup after institutions have shown their hand by pushing price through important moving averages. Former resistance MAs should begin acting as support. (source: 3 Early Entry Techniques To Improve Win Rate.md)

Entries can come from multi-day support at a rising MA, a break above a consolidation high, or a short-term pattern such as an inside day. Stops usually sit below recent lows (preferably within 3-5% of entry) or below the relevant moving averages. A Launch Pad does **not** require a prior Power of 3 trigger to be valid. (source: 3 Early Entry Techniques To Improve Win Rate.md; live article 2026-07-02)

## High Volume Close

High Volume Close works best when a stock breaks out to new highs on a positive earnings report or news reaction. The source looks for a 10%+ gap, highest volume in one year / since IPO / ever, closing range above 75%, and upward revisions to sales or EPS expectations. (source: 3 Early Entry Techniques To Improve Win Rate.md)

Entry tactics include buying near gap-day volume support, buying near the high-volume close, or entering near relevant lower-timeframe moving averages if risk can be controlled. Stops can be below day-one volume support, below the day-one low, on a close below the HVC, or on a close below a relevant intraday moving average. (source: 3 Early Entry Techniques To Improve Win Rate.md)

## Shakeout +3 (added 2026-07-02 from live scrape)

Shakeout +3 (credited to Jesse Livermore) is an early entry inside a **W-bottom / double-bottom base that forms after a prolonged uptrend**. The second low must undercut the first low — the shakeout that transfers shares from weak hands to institutions. (source: shakeout-plus-3-early-entry article)

Entry rules:

- Buy when price recovers to **$3 above the first low** for ~$30 stocks, **$6 above** for $60+ stocks, or roughly **10% above the first low** as the general form.
- This triggers well before the traditional W mid-point breakout (UBER 2023 example: ~8% before the standard pivot).
- It is an *earlier* entry, not a *looser* one: the base context (prolonged uptrend, recognizable W, quick recovery off the undercut low) must be present. "More of an art than a science." (source: shakeout-plus-3-early-entry article)

Examples cited: UBER, NVDA, ELF, SNOW (all 2023).

## Range Entries And ORB — Moglen Case-Study Doctrine (added 2026-07-02)

Richard Moglen's weekly case studies define how TraderLion actually uses intraday entries. The load-bearing rule: **the setup is identified on the daily and weekly timeframe first; intraday timeframes are only for execution.** "For ranges I typically like to go down to a 65 minute timeframe. This is AFTER I have identified the setup on a daily & weekly timeframe." (source: W range breakout case study)

Four entry tactics for trading ranges:

1. **Anticipation** — position inside the range before the move.
2. **Accumulation versus range lows** — buy tests of the lows showing support.
3. **Standard breakout** — buy the range break itself.
4. **Undercut and rally (U&R)** — price undercuts the range low, then rallies back through; Moglen's stated favorite, because it occurs on the day the stock is actually making its move.

Qualifying conditions that gate ALL four tactics:

- The stock must come from a **strong trend pulling back to the 21ema and/or a tight, defined range**. "Choppier names should be avoided." (source: W range breakout case study)
- RS leadership matters: the TEM ORB trade required weeks of visible relative strength (top of leaders screen) and a pullback to the 10ema + prior pivot confluence *before* any intraday signal counted. (source: TEM ORB case study)
- Market context is part of the setup: the best U&R/ORB days were market gap-downs that rallied off the open, giving a low-risk stop at low-of-day.
- Stops: low of the entry bar / LoD, typically **under 3% position risk**; raise to breakeven on follow-through.
- Health check: a valid ORB/U&R entry is **immediately profitable** — "Both would immediately have been profitable, the best sign for an entry." If it stalls or goes red, the signal is suspect.
- ORB timeframes used in the case studies: **5-minute** opening range for the trigger, **65-minute** for range structure. (A 15-minute opening range is a reasonable variant of the same idea; the timeframe is not the edge — the pre-qualified daily setup is.)

## Oops Reversal (added 2026-07-02 from live scrape)

The Oops Reversal (developed by Larry Williams, taught by Richard Moglen) is a short-term pullback entry tactic: the stock **gaps below the prior day's low, then rallies back through that level**. The prior day's low is the pivot; the gap-down stops out weak holders and shorts pile in, then institutions absorb the supply — the reclaim squeezes both groups back in. (source: the-oops-reversal-entry-setup-tactic article)

Context requirements (same "setup first" doctrine as all TraderLion tactics):

- Use in a **strong stock up the right side of a base or shortly after a breakout** — where institutions are still building positions.
- The gap-down cause should ideally be **market-driven** (CPI, macro news), not stock-specific. Stock-specific gaps are case-by-case.
- Best when the gap-down open lands near a key level: base pivot, 10ema, 21ema, or prior resistance — and when the stock is forming a short pullback / higher-low range.
- An Oops in an **extended** stock is a day-trade at best, not a swing entry.

Execution:

- Textbook entry: as price pushes back through the prior day's low. Stop = low of day, position risk **under ~3%**; raise the stop to below the oops pivot once ~3% above it.
- Anticipation variants on large gap-downs (4%+ from the pivot): an **opening range breakout below the oops pivot**, or a key-daily-level reclaim — with tighter stops (**under 2%** position risk). This is the one sanctioned use of an ORB *below* a daily pivot, and it still requires the daily/weekly setup to be qualified first.
- Pre-market planning is mandatory: alerts ~0.5% below the prior day's low on focus-list names only ("stocks... setup on a daily and weekly timeframe up the right side of a base or shortly in their trends after a breakout").

**Follow-through is the confirmation**: day-1 should close strong with at least ~1R (~3%) cushion, ideally near the day's high and the prior day's high (best ones are positive outside days), and the next day should continue. No follow-through = suspect signal.

### Method A — Breakout Entry

Best used when a stock has formed a **tight consolidation** (bull flag / pennant) after a big move:

1. Identify tight, coiled pattern above moving averages
2. Volume decreasing on consolidation (demand still present, supply absorbed)
3. Trigger = break above prior day's high / trendline / base resistance

4. Entry confirmation: **first 30-minute green candle** (30M pivot)
5. Entry trigger: break of the high of that 30M candle
6. Stop = low of day

## Tight Area Entry Setup

The cleanest tight-area entry is a **controlled pullback or shelf after a major base breakout**, not a random pause in a weak stock. It should appear near the upper part of the prior move, hold above key moving averages, dry up in volume, and then break a clear pivot. (source: user-provided tight-area entry setup image)

### Best Characteristics

| Characteristic | What to look for | Setup implication |
|---|---|---|
| Location | Top two-thirds of the prior range, ideally just above a big base breakout | Strength is being absorbed near highs rather than fully retracing |
| First 21ema pullback | First pullback to the 21ema after the base breakout | Best `PB-21EMA` / `MA-PULLBACK` candidate if volume contracts |
| High momentum 10ema pullback | In the fastest names, the first pullback may only reach the 10ema | Do not demand a deeper 21ema tag if the leader is holding the 10ema tightly |
| Volume | Low volume on the pullback or shelf | Supply is drying up; high-volume pullback is a warning |
| Priming pattern | Inside bar, upside reversal, positive expectation breaker, or tight setup day before the pivot | Gives a tighter pivot and cleaner stop before breakout |
| Trigger | Break above the tight-area pivot or inside-day high | Entry is the pivot breakout, not the middle of the pullback |

### Entry Checklist

1. Confirm the stock already broke out of a larger base or is in a clear momentum trend.
2. Check that the tight area forms in the upper part of the structure, not below failed resistance.
3. Prefer the **first** 21ema pullback after the base breakout; for exceptional momentum leaders, accept the first 10ema pullback.
4. Require low-volume contraction during the pullback.
5. Wait for a priming pattern: inside bar, upside reversal, positive expectation breaker, or tight setup day.
6. Enter only on the pivot break; avoid buying before the pivot if the stop would become wide or undefined.

This setup connects SRxTrades' EMA pullback method with Qullamaggie-style pivot discipline: the pullback creates the low-risk location, the priming pattern creates the pivot, and the pivot breakout supplies the trigger. It should usually classify as `MA-PULLBACK` or `PB-21EMA`; if the main feature is the larger base breakout itself, classify it as `BO-CB` or `BO-VCP` instead.

## Breakout Confirmation: Wait for Higher-Low (2026-05-07 calibration)

A 109-trade backfill (2023–2026) found that **30% of breakout entries got whipsawed below the entry-day LoD before recovering** at +14d. Diagnosis: entries are happening on day 1 of the breakout, before any post-breakout consolidation has produced a higher low — so the entry-day's low is inside the volatility envelope, not below the structural failure point.

**Updated rule** for `BO-CB` and `BO-VCP` setups (does NOT apply to `EP-FRESH`, `ORH-INTRADAY`, or `PB-21EMA`):

1. After the breakout day, **wait for at least one subsequent daily bar that prints a higher low** than the breakout day.
2. Enter on day 2+ of the breakout (or later — ORH-INTRADAY remains the day-1 alternative when intraday-only).
3. The first higher-low becomes your structural stop. It survives normal post-breakout pullbacks.

The cost: you give up the explosive day-1 follow-through trades (~20% Grade-A in the backfill). The benefit: you avoid the 30% whipsaw and the resulting damage to mental capital.

## A pullback to the rising MA is the setup forming — not weakness (2026-06-15 calibration)

A controlled pullback to the rising 8/21EMA *after* an EP or base breakout — on **contracting** volume, with RS leadership intact and price holding above the rising MAs — is the `POST-GAP-VCP` / first `PB-21EMA` **forming**, not relative weakness. Do not score it PASS for "down day / below the 8EMA / MACD falling": those are exactly what a healthy first pullback looks like (price drifts below the 8EMA toward the 21EMA as volume dries up).

Rules:

- Classify it `POST-GAP-VCP` or `PB-21EMA` / `MA-PULLBACK` and judge it on **pullback** criteria (low/contracting volume = good), not breakout criteria.
- It is a WATCH until the **trigger**: a reclaim of the 8/10EMA or a Power-of-3 / tight-area break on a **volume expansion**. Buying mid-pullback with volume still contracting and MACD still falling is catching the knife.
- Distinguish from genuine weakness: a *high-volume* pullback, a close decisively **below** the rising 21EMA, or a weak-RS name lagging a strong tape.

Example (2026-06-15): TWLO was first scored 31/PASS as "relative weakness" — a mis-read. It was the first pullback to the rising 21EMA after an earnings EP (gap to ~238 on +229% volume), on RVol ~55% contraction, RS Rating 94. Corrected to WATCH (Conviction 66): buyable on the reclaim trigger above the 8EMA, stop below the pullback low. See traderlion-early-entry-techniques (Launch Pad / Power of 3) and gapper-entry.

## An intraday trigger is not a setup — ORB/U&R doctrine (2026-07-02 calibration)

Opening-range breakouts (ORB/ORH — any timeframe: 1m/5m/15m/60m) and undercut-and-rally (U&R, "overshoot and come back") are **entry tactics**, not setups. They time an entry into a trade that already qualifies on the **daily/weekly** chart; they can never qualify the trade by themselves. TraderLion's own case studies state this explicitly: the intraday timeframe comes "AFTER I have identified the setup on a daily & weekly timeframe," and "choppier names should be avoided." (source: TraderLion TEM ORB and W range-breakout case studies, scraped 2026-07-02 — URLs in traderlion-early-entry-techniques)

**When an ORB/U&R entry is permitted:**

1. **EP day** — fresh catalyst, big gap, huge volume → ORH trigger (Qullamaggie 1m/5m/60m).
2. **Day-1 breakout from a qualified tight base** → ORH as the sniper entry (SRxTrades 5m/15m; the base must already pass tightness/volume checks).
3. **First pullback reclaim** — qualified PB-21EMA/POST-GAP-VCP, trigger = reclaim of the 8/10EMA or tight-area break on volume expansion.
4. **U&R in a tight, defined range** — stock in a strong trend, RS leader, range tight (low RMV), undercut of the range low that rallies back through it **on the day the range actually breaks**. Stop = LoD, position risk under ~3%, and the entry should be immediately profitable; if it goes red quickly the signal was false.
5. **Oops Reversal** — gap below the prior day's low that reclaims it, in a strong stock up the right side of a base / shortly post-breakout, ideally on a market-driven gap-down near a key MA. The codified "gap down and come back" tactic — full rules in traderlion-early-entry-techniques.

**When it is forbidden (auto-PASS at the pre-screen, before any rubric scoring):**

- The daily structure is **wide and loose** (repeated ±5%+ swings, alternating large up/down days) — an intraday bullish signal inside chop is noise, not a setup.
- There are recent **high-volume distribution days** in or above the range.
- The bounce/breakout day runs on **below-average volume** (RVOL < ~1 fails Jeff Sun's confirmation for any breakout-type entry).
- The "undercut" did not undercut a *meaningful* prior low of the structure, or deeper lows sit unreclaimed below (overhead supply on every side).
- Buying a mid-range bounce that has broken nothing: if price is still inside the range and below the pivot/mid-resistance, there is no trigger — same doctrine as the `NEWS-NO-TRIGGER` eval case.

Worked example (2026-07-01, GFS): after a May EP run to ~92, June printed seven ±5–11% daily swings with a 21.7M-share (-9.8%) distribution day — textbook wide-and-loose. The Jun 29 undercut to 75.58 (which never undercut the true structure low of 70.38 from Jun 9) bounced to 82.4 on 0.65x volume; a 15-minute ORB read led to a ~82 mid-range entry with a ~78 stop (<1 ATR in a 6.8%-ATR name). Jul 1 gapped down through the stop, -6.29% close. Every forbidden condition above was present; the correct classification at entry time was PASS regardless of the intraday chart. Note the Shakeout+3 comparison: even TraderLion's *early* W-bottom entry would not have triggered until ~10% above the first low (~$86+), and only in a valid W after a prolonged uptrend. See traderlion-early-entry-techniques.

## Post-EP wedge break / MA-cluster reclaim is an early entry — verify the stage before PASSing (2026-07-09 calibration)

Operator pushback (HPE, 2026-07-09) exposed three systematic errors that made the analysis PASS a valid early entry. All three are now rules.

**The pattern (early-entry lane).** A leader prints an EP (earnings gap on monster volume), then digests it for weeks in a **falling wedge / under a downtrend line** — in a high-ADR name this digestion can run **−20 to −30% off the high** and still be normal. The early entry triggers on a **3-point confirmation**:

1. **Break/reclaim of the MA cluster** (10/21EMA converged after the correction);
2. **RS line turning up** with leadership intact (true RS Rating ≥ ~90);
3. **Break of the wedge / downtrend line** drawn across the digestion highs.

This is the same family as Launch Pad / Power-of-3 in traderlion-early-entry-techniques and the `POST-GAP-VCP` reclaim — it fires **inside** the base, before the standard pivot, which is exactly why the standard gates mis-read it.

**Rule 1 — verify the stage before accepting any bounce label.** A name **above a rising 50EMA with RS Rating ≥ ~90 is a Stage-2 leader in a correction**, never a `STAGE4-BOUNCE`, regardless of what an algo pattern-labeler says (the label is computed from short-window price action and mislabels post-EP digestion). An algo stage/bounce label may only cap the Setup score after checking: 50EMA slope, price vs 50EMA, and RS. (HPE 2026-07-09: algo said STAGE4-BOUNCE/PASS; structure said RS 98, +141% above the 52-wk low, above a rising 50EMA — Stage-2 digestion.)

**Rule 2 — pattern stops, not mechanical swing-low stops.** Risk on a reclaim/wedge-break entry is measured to the **LoD of the trigger day or the wedge low** — not to the 10-day swing low. The mechanical 10d-swing-low stop inflated HPE's risk to −15.5% ("untradeable") when the pattern stop was −3.8% (LoD) to −8.5% (wedge low). A wrong stop basis silently converts a valid entry into a PASS.

**Rule 3 — volume is the deciding gate, extension is not.** A reclaim by definition happens a few percent **above** the reclaimed MA — "extended above the 10EMA" is not a rejection reason for this lane. What decides:

- Reclaim on **≤ ~1× RVOL** → *unconfirmed*: permitted only as a **half-size early entry** with the LoD stop, and the daily close must confirm (close back inside the wedge / below the MAs = exit, −0.5R, no debate).
- **RVOL ≥ 1.5× on the reclaim close** (or the subsequent pivot break) → confirmed: full size / add.
- Re-check at ~15:30 ET before carrying the trigger overnight (mid-morning strength is provisional — ALAB 2026-07-02, failed-bounce tape 2026-07-07).

**Worked example (HPE 2026-07-09).** June-1 EP (record Q2 post-Juniper, ~558% volume) → 5-week falling wedge −26% off the high → 3-point trigger day: MA-cluster break at ~$47, RS line turning (RS 98), wedge break; LoD stop $45.64 (−3.8%), first supply ~$50.5, prior high zone ~$56 → ~2–4R. Initial analysis PASSed it on the algo Stage-4 label + "extended above 10EMA" + a −15.5% swing-low stop — all three now corrected by the rules above. The one legitimate caution was volume (~1× at trigger time) → half-size early entry, volume decides the add.

A lockout-rally creates urgency because normal pullbacks do not appear. That urgency is not an entry trigger. Use one of two lanes: (source: Sean Trades lockout-rally post)

# Risk Management

**Summary**: Risk management is the set of rules and habits that prevent a bad trade from becoming a catastrophic loss, and bad luck from becoming a blown account.

**Sources**: Alex's Swing Trading System.md, Alex's Trading Psychology Reflections.md, The blueprint to consistently making $10km+ in trading.md, Post by @Clement_Ang17 on X.md

**Last updated**: 2026-07-16

---

## Core Philosophy

> "Defense is Offense" — Alex's Golden Rule

Controlling downside is not a constraint on returns — it is the *source* of returns. Traders who survive long enough compound; traders who blow up do not come back. (source: Alex's Swing Trading System.md)

## Stop Losses

### Alex's Soft Stop Approach
Stops are **structure-based**, not fixed percentages. A close below the 21dma-structure signals the trade thesis has failed. (source: Alex's Swing Trading System.md)

- Stops adjust as the stock moves: the 21dma rises → stop rises
- Do not use hard % stops (e.g. "I'll stop at -8%") — let price structure define the level

### Jeff Sun's <60% ATR LoD Rule
Entries where the stop (low of day) is within 60% of the stock's ATR allow for tight, precise risk control. This is one of Jeff's core execution disciplines. (source: The Complete Traders' Guide.md)

### SRxTrades' Low-of-Day Stop
On breakout entries, the stop is the **low of day** — creating a tight risk/reward where a small stop funds a large potential trend ride. (source: The blueprint to consistently making $10km+ in trading.md)

### Qullamaggie's Low-of-Day + ATR/ADR Guardrail
For Qullamaggie-style breakouts and episodic pivots, the initial stop is normally the **low of day**. The stop should not be wider than 1× to 1.5× ATR or ADR; if the stop is wider than the stock's normal daily range, the setup usually loses its asymmetric risk/reward. (source: Qullamaggie official setup posts)

> **Calibration note (2026-05-07, updated 2026-05-11)**: A 109-trade backfill found this strict LoD rule whipsawed **30% of trades that ultimately closed positive at +14d**. Updated guidance: keep the LoD stop strict for **fresh catalyst-day Episodic Pivots only** (the catalyst-day low is the genuine failure point), but use a structural stop for **Common Breakouts**, **VCP**, and **post-gap VCP / gapper entries**. For `POST-GAP-VCP`, risk usually belongs below the tight post-gap range, recent higher low, 10ema/21ema, or gap-day volume support. See qullamaggie-breakouts-episodic-pivots "Stop Calibration by Setup Type" and gapper-entry.

### Stop distance band by setup type — FLOOR **and** CEILING

Stops have **two** bounds, and both are hard. A floor alone (added 2026-05-07/05-11) caused the
2026-07-16 VCTR false-GO: see the calibration note below.

**Floor (anti-whipsaw).** Audit evidence across 7 months (2025-07 → 2026-05) shows the rubric
mispredicts stops ~25% of the time, always too tight. Minimum distance when computing
`predicted_stop_price`:

- `BO-CB`, `BO-VCP`, `EP-FRESH`, `POST-GAP-VCP`, `MA-PULLBACK`: minimum `1.5 × ATR(14)` below entry, OR the 5-day low, whichever is **wider** (further from entry) — **subject to the ceiling below**.
- `PB-21EMA`: anchored to 21EMA, but never tighter than `1.2 × ATR(14)`.
- `PARABOLIC`, `ORH-INTRADAY`: **no floor** — tight stops are intentional and correct for these. Never substitute a wider structural low (e.g. the 5-day low) for the tight stop; "no floor" means *keep it tight*, not *widen it*.
- `EP-SECOND`, `CONTINUATION`: minimum `1.3 × ATR(14)`.

**Ceiling (anti-untradeable-risk) — HARD GATE.** Per the Qullamaggie ATR/ADR guardrail above, the
stop must **not be wider than `1.5 × ATR(14)`** measured to the **pattern stop** (trigger-day LoD /
wedge low / base low — see entry-methods Rule 2). This is not advisory.

**When the floor exceeds the ceiling, the setup is REJECTED — you do not widen the stop.**
A structural low further than `1.5 × ATR` from entry is *information*: the stock has travelled too
far from its last real failure point, so no tight stop exists and the asymmetry is gone. Emit
`RISK-GATE-FAIL` and PASS the candidate. The floor exists to stop you entering with a *too-tight*
stop; it was never a licence to accept unbounded risk.

> **Calibration note (2026-07-16, VCTR false-GO — operator-found).** VCTR was auto-proposed ENTER at
> conviction 80 with entry 97.70 / stop 85.59 = **12.4% risk = 3.86 × ATR**, i.e. 2.6× the ceiling.
> Root cause: this section's floor rule ("whichever is **wider**") was implemented in the runtime
> (`alist-metrics.ts` `atrFloorStop`) while the ceiling in "Qullamaggie's Low-of-Day + ATR/ADR
> Guardrail" above was never ported — the two rules on this page **contradicted each other** and the
> code followed the permissive one. The system's own ATR stop (93.00) was correct and got overridden
> by the wider 5-day low. Compounding it: `PARABOLIC`/`ORH-INTRADAY` "no minimum" was implemented as
> multiplier `0` → ATR stop `null` → fell back to the 5-day low, meaning **the most extended setups
> received the widest stops** — the exact inverse of the intent. Both are now fixed here and in the
> runtime. Same root cause as the HPE 2026-07-09 miss (mechanical swing-low stop instead of a pattern
> stop) — there it produced a false PASS, here a false GO. **Risk must be measured to the pattern
> stop and gated at both ends.**

This rule is enforced for AI-generated stops in trade-analyser and `scripts/audit_trades.py`, and
at runtime in `market_dashboard` `alist-metrics.ts` (`atrFloorStop` → `RISK-GATE-FAIL`). See
rubric-stop-too-tight for the 15 historical whipsaw cases the floor saved, and
a-list-gate-and-screener for the A-list hard gate.

# Position Sizing

**Summary**: Risk-based position sizing is how traders control downside on each trade. Multiple sources converge on scaling in gradually rather than going full-size at entry.

**Sources**: Alex's Swing Trading System.md, The blueprint to consistently making $10km+ in trading.md, The Complete Traders' Guide.md, 3 Early Entry Techniques To Improve Win Rate.md

## Calibration Note (2026-05-07 backfill finding)

A 109-trade backfill found a 30% whipsaw rate on Common Breakouts — trades that closed positive at +14d but had their predicted stop breached intraday. With that base rate, per-trade risk needs to absorb a long string of false-stops without breaking the account.

**Updated default per-trade risk** (deviation from the 1% upper bound):

| Setup type | Default risk per trade |
|---|---|
| `EP-FRESH` (true Episodic Pivot, catalyst-day stop) | Up to 1.0% — the LoD is genuinely the failure point |
| `POST-GAP-VCP` (post-gap tight range / pullback entry) | 0.5% default, max 0.75% — the setup is cleaner than chasing the gap, but the structural stop can still be wider than a day-one ORH |
| `BO-CB`, `BO-VCP` (Common / VCP Breakouts) | **0.5% default**, max 0.75% — the structural stop is wider, so risk per share is bigger; lowering % keeps total exposure sane |
| `PB-21EMA` (pullback to MA) | Up to 1.0% — soft stop is reliable |
| `ORH-INTRADAY` | Up to 0.5% — short horizon, less time for thesis to play out |
| Other / OTHER | 0.25% — exploratory sizing |

Reasoning: at 30% whipsaw rate, expected loss per trade is 0.3 × stop loss. The 50% B-grade trades produce small gains; the 20% A-grades produce the +R outcomes. Lower per-trade risk preserves capital across the long string of B/C trades so the A-grades can compound. (source: `verdicts/_audit_*.md`, 2026-05-07, `wiki/_skill-suggestions.md` #006.)

## Alex's Risk-Based Framework

Alex sizes positions as a percentage of **account equity risked per trade** — not a fixed share count or dollar amount. (source: Alex's Swing Trading System.md)

| Conviction Level | Risk % per Trade |
|---|---|
| Weakness entry (less confirmation) | 0.25% of equity |
| Confirmed entry | 0.5% of equity |
| High-conviction setup | Up to 1% of equity |

**Initial allocation**: 10–20% of total risk budget per name at first entry. The remainder is added on through **scaling in** as the trade proves itself.

## Building a Position (Scale-In)

1. Start with 10–20% of intended full position
2. Add 2–3 times as confidence grows and price confirms
3. Maximum 3 adds; each add requires the stock to hold structure

This approach creates a **cushion** — early profits on the first tranche fund the risk on subsequent adds. Cushion acts as leverage without margin. (source: Alex's Swing Trading System.md)

## SRxTrades' Trimming Framework

@SRxTrades uses a 4-tranche **trim-as-you-go** approach to exit winners (source: The blueprint to consistently making $10km+ in trading.md):

| Tranche | Size | Exit Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| First trim | 25% | Sell into strength at big resistance |
| Second trim | 25% | First close below 8 EMA |
| Third trim | 25% | Break of 21 EMA |
| Final trim | 25% | Break of 50 EMA on closing basis |

This keeps exposure running for the full trend while systematically locking in gains.

## Jeff Sun's ATR%-Based Sizing

Jeff Sun uses **ADR%** (Average Daily Range) to calibrate position size — higher ADR% stocks get smaller allocations because their larger daily swings translate to bigger dollar P&L swings at the same share count. (source: The Complete Traders' Guide.md)

## Qullamaggie's Risk + Position Cap

Qullamaggie sizes from account risk first, then caps by exposure and liquidity. Most trades risk **0.25-1.0%** of account equity, typical positions are **10-20%** of account, and a single stock/ETF should not exceed **30% overnight**.

```text
shares = account_risk_dollars / (entry_price - stop_price)
position_value = shares * entry_price
```

If the formula produces a position that is too large for the account cap or the stock's liquidity, reduce size or skip the trade.

## Key Principles

- Position size is determined by **stop distance + max risk**, not gut feel
- Trim 1/3 at **2R** (twice initial risk) to lock in early profits (Alex's approach)
- Never add to a losing trade; only add when the stock is proving you right
- Cushion (unrealized gains) earned early is the best form of leverage
- Qullamaggie-style breakouts and EPs need low-of-day stops tight enough that position size can stay within account and liquidity limits

# Lockout Rally

**Summary**: A lockout rally is a fast, persistent advance that offers few normal pullbacks, leaving late buyers and sidelined cash without a comfortable entry. The tradeable response is to distinguish established leaders that need time to digest from lagging groups receiving rotation flows; neither lane justifies chasing an extended candle.

**Sources**: `(2) Sean trades on X A lot of you have probably seen the words lockout rally on your timeline a million times alreadyBUTwhat actually is a lockout rally & How can you take advantage of itA lockout rally is essentially a fast, aggressive move hig.md` ([source post](https://x.com/SRxTrades/status/2044536242319380640)); Post by @martinlukkt on X.md

**Last updated**: 2026-07-18

---

## Definition

A lockout rally is a fast, aggressive move higher with few proper pullbacks. Price gaps or pushes higher, early buyers are already positioned, shorts repeatedly try to call the top, late buyers chase, and sidelined cash waits for a dip that does not arrive. The defining feature is not simply a green market; it is the persistent absence of a low-risk re-entry. (source: Sean Trades lockout-rally post)

The term describes **market behavior**, not a buy signal. A stock holding up while the index falls supplies relative-strength evidence, but it still needs a valid setup, pivot, extension check, and pattern stop before entry.

## Two Effects Of A Lockout Rally

### 1. Capital rotates into lagging groups

When the original leaders become too extended, traders still seeking exposure may move capital into lagging industries or stocks. Sean cites software, quantum, and nuclear as examples of themes that can re-emerge during such rotation. These moves can produce fast momentum, but a rotating laggard is not automatically the market's next durable leader. (source: Sean Trades lockout-rally post)

Operational read:

- Require several peers to confirm the group move.
- Prefer the strongest stock in the group, not a weak sympathy name.
- Demand a real pivot and trigger volume.
- Use shorter expectations until the stock proves sustained institutional leadership.

### 2. Leaders correct through time instead of price

The strongest leaders may refuse to pull back deeply. Instead, they move sideways while the 8/10/21-day averages catch up and volatility contracts. This time correction can eventually produce the first proper continuation base or moving-average pullback. (source: Sean Trades lockout-rally post)

Operational read:

- Do not chase the third green candle or a vertical extension.
- Let the stock form its first tight shelf, flag, VCP, or orderly MA pullback.
- Require volume to contract during the pause and expand at the trigger.
- Enter from the new pivot or reclaim, with risk measured to the new structure.

## Execution Lanes

| Lane | What it looks like | Entry state | Main danger |
|---|---|---|---|
| Established leader / time correction | Major leader remains near highs while short-term MAs catch up | `WATCH` until a tight base, priming pattern, or MA-reclaim trigger forms | Chasing an extended leader because it looks invincible |
| Rotation candidate | Lagging group begins outperforming as capital seeks a new home | `WATCH` until peer confirmation, RS, pivot, and trigger volume appear | Confusing a short rotation trade with a new secular leader |
| Emerging Stage 2 leader | A stock begins its own Stage 2 while the index rally is already mature | Candidate-generation lane; apply the full GO gate | Projecting a historical analogue onto an unproven stock |

This connects Sean's execution framework with Martin Luk's QCOM analogy. The broad rally and an individual stock can be on different clocks, but a young stock-stage does not excuse a late entry. (source: Post by @martinlukkt on X.md)

## GO-Gate Rules

A lockout-rally candidate cannot become `GO` unless it passes the normal GO gate:

1. **Location**: not more than 2 ATR above the rising 21EMA.
2. **Structure**: a real consolidation high, reclaim level, or MA-pullback trigger exists; the latest close is not relabelled as a pivot.
3. **Volume**: contraction during the base or pullback, followed by expansion at the trigger.
4. **Risk**: the pattern stop is no wider than 1.5 ATR; if no valid stop exists, the stock is not actionable.
5. **Leadership**: RS is confirmed against the index and, where relevant, against sector peers.
6. **Catalyst**: the theme or company catalyst is source-backed rather than inferred from price alone.
7. **Market exposure**: a weak or deteriorating index reduces size even when the stock is holding up.

## Common Misreads

- **"It will not pull back, so I must buy now."** That is the emotional condition the rally creates, not an edge.
- **"Holding green in a red tape is the entry."** It is evidence for the watchlist; structure supplies the entry.
- **"Rotation means the laggard is the next leader."** Rotation can be brief and tactical.
- **"A mature index means no new leaders can emerge."** Individual stocks can begin Stage 2 later, but still require a proper base and trigger.
- **"The moving averages will catch up eventually."** Time correction is useful only if price remains constructive and volume shows supply drying up.

# Fundamental Analysis Framework (A/B/C Rating)

**Summary**: A structured scoring system for grading stocks on fundamentals — growth, profitability, valuation, and balance sheet strength — using numeric thresholds and weighted categories to produce an A/B/C rating.

**Sources**: lengyan_deep research.md

**Last updated**: 2026-05-03

---

## Overview

This framework is derived from lengyan-cold-eye's approach to stock evaluation. It provides a repeatable, evidence-based method for grading the **fundamental quality** of a stock, independent of its technical chart setup.

In Jie Sheng's trade journal, this A/B/C rating runs as a **parallel score alongside the 7-trader technical scoring** (see trader-styles). The technical score evaluates execution; the fundamental score evaluates stock selection.

---

## The Four Categories

### 1. Growth (30% weight)

Revenue and EPS growth rate over 3–5 years.

| Grade | Threshold |
|---|---|
| **A** | Average growth ≥ 15% per year |
| **B** | Average growth 5–15% per year |
| **C** | Average growth < 5% per year |

Look at the trajectory, not just the latest year. A stock recovering from a cyclical trough (e.g. TSMC 2023 → 2026) may score B on trailing data but A on forward estimates — note the discrepancy. (source: lengyan_deep research.md)

### 2. Profitability (30% weight)

ROE, net margin, and operating cash flow quality.

| Grade | Threshold |
|---|---|
| **A** | ROE ≥ 20% **and** net margin ≥ 20% |
| **B** | ROE 10–20% |
| **C** | ROE < 10% |

Cash flow matters: a company with high margins but negative operating cash flow is a red flag. CFO growth > 15%/yr = A, 5–15% = B, negative or low = C. (source: lengyan_deep research.md)

### 3. Valuation (20% weight)

P/E and P/B relative to quality.

| Grade | Threshold |
|---|---|
| **A** | P/E < 20 **and** P/B < 3 |
| **B** | P/E 20–30 **or** P/B 3–6 |
| **C** | P/E > 30 **or** P/B > 6 |

Dividend yield is a supplementary signal: yield > 3% supports an A grade; < 1% is a yellow flag for income-oriented strategies. (source: lengyan_deep research.md)

### 4. Balance Sheet (20% weight)

Debt-to-equity ratio and liquidity.

| Grade | Threshold |
|---|---|
| **A** | D/E < 0.50 **and** current ratio > 1.5 |
| **B** | D/E 0.50–1.00 **and** current ratio 1.0–1.5 |
| **C** | D/E > 1.00 **or** current ratio < 1.0 |

Net-cash companies (negative net debt) automatically score A on this dimension. (source: lengyan_deep research.md)

---

## Scoring Method

Each category earns points: **A = 2, B = 1, C = 0**.

Weighted score = (Growth × 0.30) + (Profitability × 0.30) + (Valuation × 0.20) + (Balance Sheet × 0.20)

| Weighted Score | Overall Grade |
|---|---|
| ≥ 1.6 | **A** |
| 1.0–1.59 | **B** |
| < 1.0 | **C** |

```
Example: Wuliangye (2023)
  Growth:       A (rev +12.6%, EPS +13.2%) → 2 × 0.30 = 0.60
  Profitability: A (ROE 25%, margin 36%) → 2 × 0.30 = 0.60
  Valuation:    A (P/E 11.5, P/B 3.0)    → 2 × 0.20 = 0.40
  Balance Sheet: A (D/E 0.28, CR 4.5)    → 2 × 0.20 = 0.40
  Total: 2.00 → Grade A
```

---

## Data Sources

For US stocks (Jie Sheng's primary market), pull fundamental data from:
1. **Finviz** — quick snapshot (P/E, P/B, ROE, D/E, current ratio)
2. **Yahoo Finance** — income statement, balance sheet, cash flow statement
3. **SEC filings** — 10-K / 10-Q for authoritative numbers
4. **Earnings reports** — latest quarter for growth trend

For Asian stocks (冷眼's original context), use exchange filings (SSE, SZSE, TWSE) or financial databases.

---

## Limitations

- **Sector-blind**: A high-growth tech stock and a mature utility are graded on the same thresholds. Consider adjusting thresholds by sector for more nuanced scoring.
- **Backward-looking**: Uses trailing data. A stock transitioning from growth to decline (like Moutai in 2025) may look good on last year's numbers.
- **No moat assessment**: Doesn't capture competitive advantage, management quality, or industry dynamics — purely quantitative.

# Qullamaggie Breakouts and Episodic Pivots

**Summary**: Kristjan Kullamagi (@Qullamaggie) is a momentum swing trader whose edge is built around a small number of repeatable fast-moving stock patterns: common breakouts, episodic pivots, and parabolic short/long setups. For Jie Sheng's scoring system, the most important long-side frameworks are breakouts and episodic pivots.

**Sources**: Qullamaggie official "3 timeless setups" post, Qullamaggie official "How to master Episodic Pivots" post, Qullamaggie FAQ, ChartMill episodic pivot screening notes, 3 Early Entry Techniques To Improve Win Rate.md, user-provided post-gap volatility contraction image.

**Last updated**: 2026-05-11

## Core Philosophy

Qullamaggie is not a general-purpose pattern trader. The system is about mastering a few high-velocity setups that have appeared repeatedly across decades and markets.

His main long-side edge:

1. Find stocks with exceptional recent momentum or a fresh catalyst.
2. Wait for a clean risk point instead of chasing randomly.
3. Enter through opening-range highs or daily breakout levels.
4. Define risk immediately, usually at the low of day.
5. Take partial profits quickly enough to protect the trade, then let the remainder trend with moving averages.

The goal is not a high win rate. The goal is small controlled losses plus occasional 5R, 10R, or larger winners.

---

## Setup 1: Common Breakout

### Breakout Preconditions

The stock should already be a leader.

| Requirement | Rule |
|---|---|
| Recent momentum | Top 1-2% of stocks over 1-month, 3-month, and 6-month windows |
| Prior move | A strong move higher in the prior 1-3 months, often 30-100%+ |
| Base length | Usually 2 weeks to 2 months |
| Base quality | Orderly pullback or sideways consolidation, higher lows, tightening range |
| Moving averages | Price often rides the rising 10-day and 20-day MAs, sometimes 50-day |

### Breakout Entry

Enter when price breaks out of the consolidation.

Preferred triggers:

- High of the first 1-minute candle.
- High of the first 5-minute candle.
- High of the first 60-minute candle.
- Clean daily breakout above the base if intraday precision is not used.

Anticipating the breakout is possible but lower quality and requires more experience.

### Breakout Stop

Initial stop is the low of day. The stop should not exceed **1× to 1.5×** the ATR or ADR of the stock; if the stop is wider than 1.5× the normal daily range, risk/reward is usually broken. (source: qullamaggie.com/my-3-timeless-setups-that-have-made-me-tens-of-millions/)

### Breakout Exit

Qullamaggie's default breakout management:

1. Sell 1/3 to 1/2 of the position after 3-5 days if the trade works.
2. Move the stop to breakeven after the partial.
3. Trail the rest with the 10-day or 20-day moving average.
4. For beginners, use the 10-day MA and wait for the first close below it.

### Breakout Failure Patterns

Penalize a trade when any of these appear:

- No strong prior move.
- Base is loose, wide, or messy.
- Breakout runs directly into overhead supply.
- Range is not tightening before expansion.
- Volume does not confirm the breakout.
- Entry is far above the base or late after the move.
- Stop to low-of-day is wider than ATR/ADR.

---

## Setup 2: Episodic Pivot

### Definition

An episodic pivot is a catalyst-driven repricing event. The best long EPs usually start with unexpected good news, especially earnings or guidance, that forces institutions to revalue the stock.

### Episodic Pivot Preconditions

| Requirement | Rule |
|---|---|
| Catalyst | Fresh news, earnings, guidance, major contract, regulatory event, product launch, or similar repricing event |
| Gap | Ideally 10%+ gap up |
| Volume | Huge volume near the open; best cases may trade average daily volume within the first 15-30 minutes |
| Fundamentals for earnings EP | Strong EPS/revenue growth and meaningful beat versus expectations |
| Prior chart | Preferably sideways/neglected before the event, not already extended from a multi-month run |
| Liquidity | Must be tradable at the user's intended size |

ChartMill's practical screen is less strict than Qullamaggie's pure definition, but useful for finding candidates: price above 3, adequate market cap, average volume above 300k, recent gap up, recent earnings catalyst, 5%+ price change, and volume at least 100% above the previous day.

### Episodic Pivot Entry

Identify the EP in after-hours or pre-market. Enter through opening-range highs:

- 1-minute ORH for the earliest trigger.
- 5-minute ORH for more confirmation.
- 60-minute ORH for slower confirmation.

Avoid EPs that gap straight into major resistance or fail to hold momentum after the opening range.

### Episodic Pivot Stop

Initial stop is the low of day. This keeps risk defined around the day the market repriced the stock.

### Episodic Pivot Exit

Trail with the 10-day or 20-day moving average once those averages rise above the initial stop. The specific trailing rule can be tuned by trader preference, but the key is to let genuine EP trends breathe while cutting failed EPs fast.

### EP Failure Patterns

Penalize a trade when any of these appear:

- Gap is small or catalyst is weak.
- Volume is ordinary.
- Stock already had a major 3-6 month run before the gap.
- This is a second or repeated EP in a short period.
- Stock is too illiquid for the intended size.
- Opening-range breakout fails quickly.
- Close is weak or far from the highs when the trade is meant to swing.

---

## Post-Gap VCP / Gapper Entry

This is the follow-on entry after an EP or catalyst gap has already shown the stock is special. Instead of chasing the first gap candle, wait for price to hold the gap, pull back or move sideways, tighten into a VCP-like shelf, and then break through the tight range. (source: user-provided post-gap volatility contraction image)

Best conditions:

- Catalyst gap first: earnings, guidance, contract, approval, or another real repricing event.
- Price holds most of the gap and stays above key rising moving averages.
- Pullback or shelf forms in the upper part of the post-gap structure.
- Volume diminishes during the pullback or range.
- RMV / relative measured volatility compresses toward 0, if that indicator is available.
- Breakout occurs through a clear tight range, not from the middle of the pullback.
- Risk is logical below the range low, higher low, 10ema/21ema, or gap-day volume support.

Treat this as `POST-GAP-VCP`, not as a fresh catalyst-day `EP-FRESH`. The catalyst-day low is no longer automatically the failure point; the new structural tight range defines the risk.

See gapper-entry for the full workflow and examples.

## Stop Calibration by Setup Type (2026-05-07 backfill finding)

A 109-trade backfill (2023–2026) found Qullamaggie's strict LoD stop produces a **30% whipsaw rate** on Common Breakouts — trades that ultimately closed positive at +14d but had their LoD breached intraday before recovering. The catalyst-day LoD is the genuine failure point for an Episodic Pivot, but for a Common Breakout the prior consolidation low (or the rising 21EMA) is the real structural failure point.

**Updated guidance (deviation from Qullamaggie's pure rule)**:

| Setup code | Recommended stop |
|---|---|
| `EP-FRESH` | **LoD on the catalyst day**, ≤ 1× to 1.5× ATR/ADR — keep strict (catalyst-day low is the real failure point) |
| `EP-SECOND` | Treat as breakout, not EP — use structural stop below |
| `POST-GAP-VCP` | Structural stop below the tight post-gap range, recent higher low, 10ema/21ema, or gap-day volume support. NOT the original catalyst-day LoD unless it remains nearby. |
| `BO-CB`, `BO-VCP` | **Lower of**: recent swing low (last 3–5 days) **or** 21EMA. NOT the entry-day LoD. |
| `ORH-INTRADAY` | LoD on the entry day (intraday-only horizon, no overnight) |
| `PB-21EMA` | Daily close below 21EMA |
| `PARABOLIC` | Highs of the day (short) or VWAP fail |

(source: `verdicts/_audit_*.md` aggregation, 2026-05-07. Full finding in `wiki/_skill-suggestions.md` #006.)

## Risk and Position Sizing

| Rule | Guideline |
|---|---|
| Account risk | Usually 0.25-1.0% per trade; rarely more than 1% |
| Small account risk | Can be 0.5-1.5% early on, but only with strict stop discipline |
| Position size | Most positions are 10-20% of account size |
| Single-name overnight cap | Do not hold more than 30% of account overnight in one stock or ETF |
| Stop basis | Low of day for breakouts and EPs; stop should not exceed 1× to 1.5× ATR/ADR |
| Liquidity cap | Reduce size or skip if the stock cannot absorb the position cleanly |

Position sizing formula:

```text
shares = account_risk_dollars / (entry_price - stop_price)
position_value = shares * entry_price
```

Then cap the result by max position size and liquidity.

## Qullamaggie Scoring Rubric

Use the same 10-point trade-review structure:

| Dimension | Range | Qullamaggie interpretation |
|---|---:|---|
| Entry Quality | 0-4 | ORH/daily breakout from a clean base or EP; not late; stop-to-LOD within 1× to 1.5× ATR/ADR |
| Risk Management | 0-3 | Risk 0.25-1%, position 10-20%, max 30% overnight, no liquidity mismatch |
| Setup Alignment | 0-3 | True breakout or true EP with strong prior/catalyst context, volume, and clear air |

### High Score

Score 9-10 when:

- Prior move or catalyst is obvious.
- Base/EP is clean.
- Volume confirms.
- Entry is through an appropriate ORH or breakout level.
- Stop is low-of-day and not wider than ATR/ADR.
- Position size respects risk and liquidity.
- There is clear air above or no immediate overhead supply.

### Low Score

Score 0-4 when:

- Trade is a random chase, not one of the setups.
- Stop is undefined or too wide.
- Entry is late after the breakout.
- Breakout occurs into supply.
- EP has weak catalyst/volume.
- Position size ignores account risk or liquidity.

## Visual Fingerprints of an EP (for chart annotation)

Used by `pro-trader-chart-annotation` and `trade-analyser` skills to identify *where on the chart* an EP is. Without these visual anchors, the AI tends to confuse random gap candles for EPs.

### Confirming visual cues

- **Earnings 'E' marker** at the bottom of the candle (TradingView default earnings marker)
- **Volume bar 3–5× the 50-day average**; ideally the stock trades its average daily volume in the first 15–20 minutes after the open (source: qullamaggie.com/how-to-master-a-setup-episodic-pivots/)
- **Gap-up candle ≥ 10%** that closes in the upper third of its daily range
- **Prior 1–3 months sideways or neglected**; best when the stock has gone sideways for 3–6 months or more before the catalyst
- **Catalyst confirmable in news**: earnings beat (preferably mid/high or triple-digit EPS & revenue growth), FDA approval, major contract, guidance raise, regulatory event

### Disqualifying visual cues (failure patterns)

- **'E' icon followed by gap that closes in the lower half** of the day's range — no volume confirmation despite catalyst
- **Volume bar only 1–2× average** — no institutional repricing
- **'E' icon AFTER a multi-month run** already up 30%+ from a previous EP — this is a "second EP" / already extended; failure rate higher and the move probably won't be as big (source: qullamaggie.com/how-to-master-a-setup-episodic-pivots/)
- **Gap-up that immediately rolls over** and breaks opening-range low
- **Biotech catalysts** — Qullamaggie notes these are "the most difficult" despite his biomedicine background
